Steve Jobs

Early Life

Steven Paul Jobs was born on 24 February 1955 in San Francisco, California. He was adopted shortly after birth by Paul and Clara Jobs, whom he always considered his true parents. His biological parents—Abdulfattah Jandali (a Syrian graduate student) and Joanne Schieble—later had a daughter, Mona Simpson, whom Jobs met as an adult. Jobs grew up in Mountain View, in what later became Silicon Valley. From an early age, he displayed curiosity, creativity, and a rebellious streak. He enjoyed tinkering with electronics alongside his adoptive father, who helped nurture his interest in technical craftsmanship.

Education and Early Interests

    Jobs attended:
  • Montessori preschool, where his independent learning style first appeared.
  • Crittenden Middle School, where he faced bullying.
  • Homestead High School in Cupertino, where he met Steve Wozniak, a genius electronics hobbyist who would change his life.
    • Jobs also developed an interest in:
  • Electronics
  • Calligraphy
  • Eastern spirituality
  • Counter-culture movements
  • Music and philosophy

These influences later shaped Apple’s design philosophy.

College and Spiritual Journey

In 1972, Jobs enrolled at Reed College in Oregon, an expensive liberal arts school. He dropped out after one semester but continued auditing classes, especially calligraphy, which influenced Mac’s typography.

    During this period he:
  • Experimented with LSD
  • Embraced meditation and Zen Buddhism
  • Adopted minimalist living
  • Spent months in India seeking spiritual enlightenment
He later said these experiences deeply shaped his creativity and worldview.

Apple's Founding(1976)

In 1974, Jobs returned to California, working briefly at Atari. He partnered with Steve Wozniak, who had engineered a powerful personal computer. On 1 April 1976, Jobs, Wozniak, and Ron Wayne founded Apple Computer in the Jobs family garage.

Apple I (1976)

Wozniak built it; Jobs marketed it. It was the first step in making computers accessible to everyday people.

Apple II (1977)

One of the first mass-produced personal computers. A commercial breakthrough, pulling Apple into global recognition.

NeXT and Pixar

NeXT (1985)

Jobs founded NeXT, a company focused on advanced workstations for education and business.
    NeXT innovations included:
  • Object-oriented programming frameworks
  • NeXTSTEP operating system (later became macOS, iOS, iPadOS)
  • Although NeXT struggled commercially, its software would later power the future of Apple.

Pixar (1986)

Jobs bought a struggling graphics division from Lucasfilm for $10 million and turned it into Pixar Animation Studios.
    Pixar produced:
  • Toy Story (1995) — the world’s first feature-length 3D animated film
  • Later hits: Finding Nemo, The Incredibles, Cars, Up, WALL-E
  • Jobs became a billionaire through Pixar’s success.
Pixar was acquired by Disney in 2006, making Jobs the largest individual shareholder in Disney.

Death

Health Struggles

Jobs was diagnosed with a rare form of pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor in 2003. He initially refused conventional treatment, causing health complications later. Despite surgeries and medical leave, he continued to lead Apple during its most creative years.

Death

Steve Jobs passed away on 5 October 2011 at age 56, surrounded by his family. His death prompted worldwide tributes, with many recognizing him as one of the greatest innovators in technology and business history.

Bill Gates

Early Life

William Henry Gates III, widely known as Bill Gates, was born on October 28, 1955, in Seattle, Washington, USA. He grew up in an upper-middle-class family. His father, William H. Gates Sr., was a prominent attorney, and his mother, Mary Maxwell Gates, was a schoolteacher, businesswoman, and the first female director of First Interstate Bank and United Way International. Gates displayed early brilliance in mathematics and logical thinking. At age 13, he enrolled in the elite Lakeside School, which had a computer terminal—rare at the time. Gates became fascinated with programming, often spending long hours coding and experimenting. He wrote his first computer program—a simple tic-tac-toe game—on a GE computer at age 13. There, he met Paul Allen, who would later become his Microsoft co-founder. Together, Gates, Allen, and a few friends formed the “Lakeside Programmers Club,” taking on real programming jobs for local companies while still in school. By age 17, Gates and Allen developed a traffic-counting software called “Traf-O-Data,” earning their first entrepreneurial experience.

Education

In 1973, Gates entered Harvard University, where he studied pre-law briefly but then switched to mathematics and computer science. At Harvard, he befriended Steve Ballmer (future Microsoft CEO) and spent much of his time programming. In 1974, Gates and Allen discovered the Altair 8800, one of the earliest personal computers. Seeing its commercial potential, they developed a BASIC interpreter for the machine. Their success convinced Gates to drop out of Harvard in 1975 to pursue a business career.

Founding and Building Microsoft

In 1975, Gates and Allen founded Microsoft in Albuquerque, New Mexico. Their vision was to place “a computer on every desk and in every home.” Microsoft initially licensed its BASIC interpreter and later expanded into operating systems.

Major Milestones at Microsoft

  • 1980: Microsoft struck a deal with IBM to provide an operating system. Gates purchased QDOS for $50,000, modified it, and delivered it as MS-DOS, which became the industry standard.
  • 1985: Microsoft launched Windows 1.0, introducing a graphical user interface.
  • 1990: Release of Windows 3.0, a major commercial success.
  • 1995: Launch of Windows 95, one of the most influential operating systems in history.
  • Late 1990s: Microsoft dominated the PC software industry, leading to antitrust investigations in the U.S. and Europe.
  • 2000: Gates stepped down as CEO, becoming chief software architect while Ballmer became CEO.
Gates became a billionaire at age 31 and the world’s richest man multiple times due to Microsoft’s success.

Philanthropy and Global Impact

In 2000, Gates founded the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, one of the largest charitable foundations in world history. Its priorities include global health, education, poverty reduction, vaccine programs, and climate innovation.

Major Philanthropic Contributions:

  • Funding vaccines to eradicate diseases such as polio and malaria.
  • Supporting education initiatives in the U.S.
  • Building sanitation and clean water systems in developing countries
  • Investing in climate technologies and research.
  • Supporting COVID-19 relief and development of vaccine distribution systems

Personal Life

Bill Gates married Melinda French in 1994, and the couple had three children. In 2021, they announced their divorce but continued their joint leadership of the Gates Foundation.
Bill Gates is alive as of 2025 and continues to work actively on global health, climate innovation, and philanthropy.
